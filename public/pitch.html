Sending money to India is expensive, slow, and opaque
The US → India corridor is $32B/year. The experience hasn't kept up.

Wise is more honest at 0.8–1.3%, but still charges an explicit fee on top; Remitly buries the markup in the exchange rate with no disclosure
SWIFT-based transfers take 1–3 business days with unpredictable correspondent bank fees that vary per transaction
330,000 Indian students in the US — the single largest international student group — send money home regularly: family support, account repayments, personal savings
Most apps force users to understand FX mechanics or choose between confusing payment rails — high friction, high drop-off

Stablecoin rails. Fiat UX.
The infrastructure that makes the rate advantage structurally sustainable.
On-ramp (US): Licensed MSB partner receives USD wire, converts to USDC on Stellar
Settlement: USDC on Stellar settles in ~6 seconds, globally, at near-zero network cost
Off-ramp (India): RBI/FEMA-compliant partner converts USDC → INR and credits the Indian bank account
Borderless never holds funds — orchestration and UX only. No money transmission licence required.
